# FloorCalc Pro Fact-Check Audit

Date: 2026-05-26

## Scope

Audited the current live FloorCalc Pro content set:

- 54 live guide records across planning, LVP/LVT, laminate, hardwood and engineered hardwood, carpet and padding, tile, transitions, and troubleshooting.
- 6 live calculator pages.
- Guide and calculator rendering for disclaimers, planning language, and external reference support.

No new guide routes were added in this pass.

## Industry Alignment Sources Added

Added compact reference support for the main flooring categories:

- National Wood Flooring Association: NWFA Technical Guidelines for hardwood, engineered hardwood, acclimation, moisture, concrete, and jobsite review.
- Carpet and Rug Institute: CRI 104/105 installation standards for carpet installation, seams, stretching, cushion, and layout planning.
- Tile Council of North America: TCNA Handbook resources for ceramic, porcelain, stone tile, substrate preparation, wet-area concerns, movement, grout joints, and workmanship.
- Resilient Floor Covering Institute: resilient flooring technical resources for LVP, LVT, sheet vinyl, manufacturer instructions, and installation considerations.
- ASTM F710: concrete floor preparation principles for resilient flooring, especially moisture, pH, cleanliness, smoothness, and bond-related concerns.
- North American Laminate Flooring Association: laminate consumer guidance around subfloors, underlayment, moisture-sensitive rooms, and product requirements.

The guide template now displays a short "Industry References & Further Reading" section when a guide matches relevant materials or conditions.

## Factual Corrections and Safer Wording

Softened wording around product and warranty implications:

- Replaced calculator wording that implied manufacturer warranties may set material quantity with safer "manufacturer ordering guidance" language.
- Replaced transition wording about "warranty coverage" with "product installation requirements."
- Renamed the transition guide section from "Check warranty requirements" to "Check product requirements."
- Updated shared guide disclaimers to emphasize product-specific installation requirements instead of warranty-related language.

Existing guide language already used cautious phrasing for:

- Waterproof and water-resistant laminate claims.
- Carpet seam visibility expectations.
- Hardwood acclimation and moisture readings.
- Engineered hardwood over concrete and moisture barriers.
- Radiant heat compatibility.
- LVP/LVT substrate flatness and underlayment requirements.
- Tile substrate flatness, mortar support, and movement concerns.

## References Added by Page Type

Reference sections are now added dynamically:

- Hardwood and engineered hardwood guides receive NWFA references.
- Carpet and padding guides receive CRI references.
- Tile guides receive TCNA references.
- LVP, LVT, and sheet vinyl guides receive RFCI references, with ASTM F710 added when concrete, slabs, subfloors, moisture, or flatness are central to the page.
- Laminate guides receive NALFA references.
- Calculator pages receive references where the tool touches installation-sensitive topics, such as carpet seams, pattern repeat, stairs, waste, and transitions.

## Calculator and Diagram Review

Calculator language remains clearly framed as planning/estimate support:

- Carpet Seam Planner states it is for broadloom carpet layout planning and not exact final seam placement.
- Pattern Repeat Calculator states it is a planning estimate and should be reviewed by the installer.
- Transition Estimator states profiles and product requirements vary.
- Stair Flooring Calculator warns that landings, stair noses, returns, stringers, pattern direction, and stair parts can change the order.
- Carton/box conversion is labeled as optional and product-label dependent.

No calculator math changes were made in this pass.

## Pages Needing Future Manual Review

These areas should get periodic manual review because guidance varies significantly by product, local conditions, or installer method:

- Radiant heat compatibility and startup procedures.
- Engineered hardwood over concrete, especially below-grade slabs and glue-down systems.
- LVP/LVT over concrete or tile, especially moisture and telegraphing concerns.
- Laminate waterproof/water-resistant claims.
- Carpet seam placement for patterned carpet or complex connected rooms.
- Tile movement joints, deflection, wet-area assemblies, and substrate-specific installation methods.
- Transition requirements for floating floors and long runs.

## Notes

This pass adds trust signals and safer wording without turning FloorCalc Pro into a technical standards manual. The site should continue to describe industry concepts in homeowner-friendly language while pointing users to authoritative resources and product instructions for final requirements.
